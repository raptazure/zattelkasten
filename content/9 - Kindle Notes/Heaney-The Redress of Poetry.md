The poem, in fact, is a showing forth of the way that poetry brings human existence into a fuller life. It is obviously less extravagant in its rhetoric than, say, Rilke’s ‘Sonnets to Orpheus’, but it is no less fully alive to the excitements and transformations which poetic activity promotes.
