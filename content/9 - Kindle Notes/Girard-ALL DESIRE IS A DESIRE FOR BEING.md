Thus we have two perspectives in Proust and other great novels of novelistic conversion. The first perspective is the deceptive perspective of desire which is full of illusions regarding the possibility of the hero to fulfill himself through desire. It is the perspective that imprisoned him in a sterile process of jumping from one frustrated desire to the next over a period of many years.
