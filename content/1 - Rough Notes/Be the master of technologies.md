### Introduction

In today's world of booming technologies, we are facing a serious problem - how to effectively use the technology so that we can become its master instead of its slave?

### Looking back

I believe many people have similar experience like me: in a dark room, lying prostrate on the bed, scrolling the phone endlessly, not doing anything  particularly, I am merely trying to extend the length of the day, in vain. I found this bad habit particularly pernicious, for it harms both our eyes and brain. Now, I decide to change.

### Moving forward 

In order to master technology instead of being mastered by it, we need to pay attention to everyday details, the so-called "minute particulars", to quote Blake's words, for there lies the secrets of habit formation. 

For me, desktop OS (Windows/MacOS/Linux) means productivity, while Android/iOS means consumption, basically because of the difference of interaction model and application ecosystem. On a computer, one can simply do more things, and the muti-tasking capacity is also considerably higher than a phone; one is more inclined to use keyboard and trackpad/mouse, which makes the act of using the device more conscious than scrolling and tapping almost unconsciously on a phone.

Next comes the question - what kind of computer then? In order to adapt to various scenarios, the device should be versatile and light, elegantly designed and enjoyable to use; ideally, it should be both a computer and a mobile device, so that most scenarios that require a touchscreen can also be covered. Then, the answer seems obvious to me - Surface Pro, which is both a computer and a tablet, with the detachable keyboard cover and attachable digital pen making it one of the most versatile devices available. And, most games are not suitable to run on this device, which can be a good thing for productivity.

Of course, choosing what kind of technologies we are using is just a beginning, what comes next may be more important - use the device to experience and to create more beautiful and meaningful things.

### Conclusion

Now, I'm typing on the Obsidian software of a Surface Pro. This a new beginning for me to change how to use technologies for better.
